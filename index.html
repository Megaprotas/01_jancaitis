Our History

Year 1967 – it was a regular winter Friday night in St.Louis, Missouri when Jack McNeil entered a colonial style house on Whatington Avenue. Not far where river Missouri tried to brake the ice that halted it’s flow. So, the place was called “Friendly Jebediah’s” owned by Jeb Harrington, locally known guitarist. Dim spotlights pointed to the guy who was testing the drums, it appeared to be Johnny “Lucky” Williams. “God dammit he’s bloody good” thought Jack. And whatever power pushed him on a stage where he picked up electric guitar and joined Lucky. Surprisingly drummer just smiled and joined. Tune was “Over the river was a town”… or a camp. Well known country song in Missouri and surrounding states. Half empty bar just wowed and joined singling new rock version of old song. Quite unexpected for sleepy place like this where young musicians just give their first tryouts. Thought it’s not the end – Jeb, jumped over the counter picked up microphone. Voice was a bit rusty as he did not had singing practice in last eight months. And this is how it began. Guys became real band constant jamming in Jack’s dad’s garage and a talent of songwriting was a reason of birth of The Bandos.

When time came to record first album, guys came to local studio where they saw a owner sleeping on a couch. It was Larry Chamberlain a future bassist and troublemaker. As for talented musician he filled the hole in Bandos’ music and with his connections he helped to have a few official gigs around Missouri, where they were spotted by Mike Carlisle, producer of Fictional Records. It was a pick up of our career when possibility to play around various venues in USA. First memorable was in “Tobia’s place” in Atlantic City, NJ, attended by 5.000 people. Since then there was no state or a city visited by the band. It lead to new searches of sound and, therefore, new albums. That meant one thing – we were going international. Trial tour was arranged in UK and started in Roundhouse, as it reached success and positive feedback – it lead to series all across the country (all the way to Glasgow) and even across La Manche. Several concerts were held in Paris, Amsterdam, Brussels and Rome. Halls were filled and that encouraged Neil, Jack, Jeb and Larry to come back to old continent several times. That is just a bit of the amazing career.Many more albums, singles were released and even more venues filled with cheering people.
